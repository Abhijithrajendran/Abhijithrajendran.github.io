I'm just a **happy person**. That's honestly the most accurate thing I can tell you about myself. I look for the green and colorful parts of life, even when things get messy, and they do get messy...! I fall. I fail. But I have this thing where I refuse to stay down, not because I'm stubborn, but because I genuinely believe things work out if you keep going. It's like our guy Santiago from Hemingway's _The Old Man and the Sea_.I'm aspirational like that. I have this glorious delusion that things will work out for me eventually..Big dreams, for myself and honestly, actually for the world too.


**Educational & Employment Background**
I've a bachelors degree in mechanical engineering from Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, but life had other plans. I started my career as a procurement specialist working for a family run architectural firm in Hyderabad where I managed procurement profolios worth $1M+, analyzed procurement spend, designed and implemented supplier performance, streamlined the vendor onboarding process and conducted inventory demand analysis using Excel (pivot tables, VLOOKUP), Power BI, Tableau, SAP Analytics. I had foundational level understanding on these softwares and platforms, but otherwise my education was mostly through courses online and with practice and daily use was able to get expert level proficiency on each. After the company shut down operations, I had a brief stint as a Verification Associate at Absolute People Screen Pvt Ltd, where I put my Excel and SQL skills to work and brought an analytical approach to background verification operations, built a real-time Tableau dashboard for monitoring verification activity and developed structured verification workflows using SQL and Excel, replacing ad hoc approaches with a repeatable, data-driven process that improved case accuracy by 25% and cut turnaround time by 30%. After my brief stint at APS Pvt Ltd, I was working in customer service at [24]7.ai and then Amazon, which at the time felt like a detour but turned out to be one of the best educations I've ever had. I spent years talking to people on some of their worst days. Angry, frustrated, let down. What I learned there is that every business in the world is really just a people problem. If you don't have customers, you've no business. That's it. It's as simple as that. Over the years, I grew through three levels over nearly five years, from frontline execution to strategic analysis and team leadership. from a Digital Interaction Associate to an executive to a specialist. Led a team of 10 customer service representatives, setting performance KPIs, translating service metrics into actionable coaching, and managing team progress toward departmental targets. At some point I started noticing patterns. There was a period where our team was drowning in DSAT scores, customer dissatisfaction, and nobody quite knew why. I got handed a massive data file and was told to figure it out. And I did. Found the patterns, traced them back to their source, coached the team, watched things change. At Amazon, I was both an operational analyst and a cross-continental process leader, conducting weekly training sessions for support teams across two geographies (US and India), communicating process updates, system changes, and escalation protocols in a structured and consistent format that reduced the knowledge gaps and improved the first-contact resolution. Circling back on all the experience, I had a moment, and I thought, okay, **this is what I want to do**. So I moved to Toronto, did a business management course, and now I'm here : learning every day, building skills, looking for work where I can actually solve things that matter.


When I'm not buried in work, I'm reading: mostly fiction. I used to be a heavy non-fiction reader until it dawned on me that fiction is actually where the real truth lives. Between the lines, in the things characters don't say..As Ben Okri writes in _A Way of Being Free_ - "Beware the stories you read or tell; subtly, at night, beneath the waters of consciousness, they are altering your world." I write too, mostly essays, fiction, journal entries. If I feel confident I might post my writings here someday. Writing, for me, is just thinking out loud with better posture. 


**If you value curiosity, warmth, empathy, and you're compassionate and someone who genuinely gives a damn, you should reach out. I think we'd get along!**
